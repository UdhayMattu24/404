What is Plastic?

Plastic is a synthetic material made from polymers,
primarily derived from petrochemicals,
Known for its versatility, durability,
and lightweight nature, plastic is used widely in packaging,
consumer goods, construction, and many other industries,
It has become a ubiquitous part of modern life,
but its resilience and slow degradation rate contribute to environmental pollution.




What is Recycling?

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into reusable materials.
This reduces the need for new raw materials, conserves energy,
and mitigates pollution by reducing waste sent to landfills or oceans,
Recycling helps manage resources sustainably, creating a circular economy.




What is Plastic Recycling?

Plastic recycling involves collecting, sorting,
processing, and remanufacturing used plastic products into new materials,
Since plastic is not biodegradable, recycling it reduces environmental damage ,
The process can vary based on the type of plastic,
as different plastics have different properties and recycling capabilities.




What is the Biggest Issue Stopping the Recycling of Plastics?

One of the biggest issues hindering plastic recycling is contamination,
When plastics are contaminated with food residues, mixed with non-recyclable materials,
or improperly sorted, they become harder and more costly to recycle,
Contaminated plastics often end up in landfills or
incinerators rather than being recycled into new products.




What is the Estimated Growth of the Plastic Recycling Market?

The plastic recycling market has been projected to grow significantly,
driven by the rising demand for sustainable practices,
Some estimates indicate an annual growth rate of around 6% to 8% for the
plastic recycling market, with the market expected to reach a multi-billion dollar valuation over the next decade.
